What is Free Open AGI?

Free Open AGI represents the culmination of artificial intelligence research - an Artificial General Intelligence that can understand, learn, and perform any intellectual task that a human can, with the crucial distinction that it's freely available to all humanity.

Free

Truly free means no barriers to access - no paywalls, no usage restrictions, no required registrations. Anyone with an internet connection can use it without cost.

Open

Completely open means all model weights, architecture details, and training methodologies are publicly available. No black boxes - transparency enables trust and improvement.

AGI

Artificial General Intelligence represents systems that can understand or learn any intellectual task that a human being can, rather than being limited to narrow domains.

Milestones on Our Journey

1950 (2%)

Turing Test Proposed

Alan Turing introduces the concept of a test for machine intelligence in his paper "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," laying the philosophical foundation for AI.

1989 (5%)

Backpropagation Revolution

Yann LeCun successfully applies backpropagation to train convolutional neural networks for recognizing handwritten digits, proving neural networks' practical potential.

2001 (7%)

Open Source Movement

The release of the first version of the Creative Commons licenses enables easier sharing of research and code, accelerating collaborative AI development.

2012 (9%)

AlexNet Breakthrough

AlexNet's dramatic win in the ImageNet competition demonstrates the power of deep learning with GPUs, sparking the modern AI revolution.

2015 (11%)

TensorFlow Released

Google open-sources TensorFlow, making powerful machine learning tools accessible to everyone and standardizing neural network development.

2017 (15%)

Transformer Architecture

The "Attention Is All You Need" paper introduces transformers, which become the foundation for nearly all subsequent major AI advances.

2020 (18%)

GPT-3 Released

OpenAI's GPT-3 demonstrates remarkable capabilities of large language models, though initially kept proprietary.

2022 (23%)

ChatGPT Launch

The release of ChatGPT makes AI assistants mainstream, proving massive public demand for AGI-like systems.

2023 (26%)

LLaMA Leak

Meta's release of Llama weights (though initially restricted) supercharges the open large language model movement.

2024 (32%)

Qwen2.5-72B

Qwen becomes the first open model to surpass GPT-4o on LiveBench, proving open weights could achieve leading performance.

2025 (39%)

DeepSeek R1

DeepSeek releases R1, matching OpenAI-o1 capabilities at 1/50th the cost - the DeepSeek moment for open AGI.

Future

Free Open AGI
