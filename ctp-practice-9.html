Requires BOTH sides to be `True` for the whole expression to be `True`. Example: `True and True` is `True`, but `True and False` is `False`. Requires AT LEAST ONE side to be `True` for the whole expression to be `True`. Example: `True or False` is `True`, but `False or False` is `False`. REVERSES the boolean value. It takes one value and flips it. Example: `not True` becomes `False`, and `not False` becomes `True`.
